Celadon is one of those glazes that looks simple and absolutely is not. The color
lives in a narrow window — too much iron and it goes murky, too little and it's just
a pale wash.

This round I ran three test tiles with small tweaks to the iron content and fired
them in reduction. The middle tile landed closest to the soft, watery green I keep
picturing, so that's the one I'll scale up next.

A few things I want to remember for next time:

- Thicker application pools beautifully in the carved lines — worth exaggerating those.
- The clay body matters as much as the glaze; the porcelain tiles read cooler and cleaner.
- Patience. Reduction rewards a slow, steady climb far more than a rushed one.

More to come once I glaze a full set of bowls with it.
